Formula (from classical vibration theory):
M(r) = 1 / √[ (1 − r²)² + (2ζr)² ]

where r = ω/ωₙ (frequency ratio), ζ = damping ratio.
At resonance (r = 1): M = 1/(2ζ) — amplification depends entirely on damping.
Low damping (ζ = 0.05) → M ≈ 10×. Typical pump bearing housing: ζ ≈ 0.05–0.15.
