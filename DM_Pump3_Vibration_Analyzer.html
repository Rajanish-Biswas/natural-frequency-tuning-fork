Understanding the Analysis Columns

Dominant Frequency — the single frequency that best explains both your velocity (mm/s) and displacement (µm) readings together. It tells you where the vibration energy is concentrated. Since a real machine has many frequencies mixed, this is the strongest one dominating the broadband reading.


Closest Harmonic — compares your dominant frequency to the pump's running speed harmonics: 1× = 24.58 Hz, 2× = 49.17 Hz, 3× = 73.75 Hz. Matching a harmonic narrows down the mechanical cause.

What picture does this table show you? Without a spectrum analyser you only see a single broadband number (e.g. 2.2 mm/s). This table reverse-engineers the dominant frequency from the velocity + displacement pair, then maps it to a likely fault mechanism. You can't distinguish unbalance from resonance from misalignment by velocity alone — the dominant frequency is the key that separates them.
